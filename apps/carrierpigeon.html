CarrierPigeon

A modern successor to paired-phone tethering. CarrierPigeon lets your desktop (macOS or Windows) route through your paired phone (iOS or Android) over an efficient WireGuard tunnel — no double-NAT, no extra battery drain, and fully under your control.

Overview

CarrierPigeon is a two-part app: a desktop companion that runs on your Mac or Windows PC, and a phone companion that runs on your paired iPhone or Android device. Together they open a WireGuard tunnel between the two devices — over Personal Hotspot Wi-Fi, USB tether, or Bluetooth PAN — and hand your desktop's traffic off through the phone's native networking stack.

The result is a lean, efficient connection between devices you already own: low latency, aggressive DNS leak protection, and screen-off battery efficiency — without the double-NAT baggage of legacy tethering software.

How it works

  • On iOS, the companion terminates a WireGuard server inside an NEPacketTunnelProvider, then forwards flows out through the phone's normal networking path. On Android, the equivalent is a VpnService bound to a foreground service.
  • The desktop companion (macOS or Windows) configures the local WireGuard client and locks DNS to a safe resolver to prevent leaks.
  • Flows are re-originated at Layer 4 on the phone so each desktop connection travels as a fresh socket with clean routing — keeping things responsive and predictable.

What CarrierPigeon is not

CarrierPigeon is not a commercial VPN service. We do not operate exit nodes, do not terminate your traffic on DeadStick-owned servers, and do not anonymize you on the public internet. Your exit point is your own phone on your own cellular plan. CarrierPigeon is fully under your control, which is why it's your responsibility to use it in line with your carrier's terms of service.

VPN scope and disclosures

CarrierPigeon uses Android's VpnService API and iOS's NEPacketTunnelProvider API for a single purpose: to route the desktop companion's outbound traffic through the paired phone's own networking stack. We do not inspect, log, collect, share, or transmit the contents, destinations, or metadata of any traffic that flows through the tunnel. The tunnel carries only the user's own traffic between two devices that the same user controls, and the app does not modify traffic from other apps on the phone.

As required by Apple's App Store Review Guideline 5.4 and Google Play's VPN policy, DeadStick Digital LLC does not sell, use, or disclose to third parties any data collected by CarrierPigeon for any purpose. The first time the tunnel activates, the app surfaces a prominent disclosure describing exactly what the tunnel does.

How your data is handled

CarrierPigeon never sees the contents, destinations, or metadata of your traffic — that traffic flows directly between your two devices and then out of your phone's own networking stack. Local configuration (WireGuard keys, paired-device identifiers) is stored on your devices in the system keychain. See the privacy policy for the complete treatment.
